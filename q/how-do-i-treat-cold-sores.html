How do I treat cold sores?

SkinAlpha Team · ·

What is the best treatment for cold sores?

Cold sores are caused by the herpes simplex virus, most commonly herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV1). A majority of the adult population have been exposed to this virus at some point in their lives.

In many individuals, previous infection with the herpes simplex virus has no consequences, however in some individuals it can lie dormant in nerve cells and periodically reactivate leading to cold sores. Since the reactivate via nerves, the appearance of the sore is preceded by a tingling or burning sensation.

Whilst a nuisance, cold sores are not dangerous to normal individuals, however they are more of a concern in patients taking immunosupressive medication or with eczema where they have the potential to spread causing more significant infection.

Treatments are as follows:-

  • Topical aciclovir: When applied at the time that the sensation of burning or tingling arises this can shorten the duration of the cold sore
  • Oral aciclovir: Aciclovir is an antiviral medication, it is taken 5 times daily for 5-7 days. Similar to topical aciclovir it should be started at the first signs of a cold sore to shorten the duration of treatment.
  • Prophylactic aciclovir: Some patients, particularly those that are immunosupreessed, can experience recurrent cold sore infections. In this situation a low dose (usually twice a day) of aciclovir can be effective in preventing active infection.
